### Apple Pays $250M for Overselling AI Features to iPhone Buyers

Apple agreed to shell out $250 million to settle a class-action lawsuit from iPhone 15 and 16 buyers who were sold on 'Apple Intelligence' that turned out to be more artificial than intelligent. Buyers who fell for the hype between June 2024 and March 2025 will get $25 to $95 each — roughly the price of admitting your voice assistant still can't set a timer reliably. Apple, naturally, admitted no wrongdoing, because nothing says 'we're focused on innovation' like writing a nine-figure check to make lawyers go away.

### EU Bans Sexualised AI Deepfakes, Explicitly Names 'Nudifier' Apps

European lawmakers struck a deal today to ban AI systems that generate sexualised deepfakes, including so-called 'nudifier' applications, embedding the prohibition directly into the EU's AI Act. For once, ' Brussels bureaucracy moves slowly' turned into 'Brussels bureaucracy moved before Silicon Valley self-regulated,' which might be the most shocking headline of all.
