### Judge Approves $1.5B Anthropic Settlement Over Pirated Books Used to Train Claude

A federal judge approved a $1.5 billion settlement requiring Anthropic to pay roughly 482,000 authors about $3,000 each for using pirated books to train Claude. About 91% of the covered works have been claimed, making it the largest known copyright recovery in history. It turns out 'move fast and break things' also applies to reading lists.

### Jack Dorsey's Buzz Puts Team Chat, AI Agents, and Git in One Place

Block's Jack Dorsey launched Buzz, an open-source workspace that merges team chat, AI agents, Git hosting, and workflows under a single identity system built on cryptographically signed Nostr events. The pitch is one self-hostable app where people and agents collaborate on code. If Slack and GitHub had a baby, and that baby was raised by crypto bros, you'd get Buzz.

### U.S. Threatens Sanctions on Chinese AI Models Over IP Theft

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said the U.S. will scrutinize open-source models from China and sanction companies if they find evidence of IP theft. The warning comes as Moonshot's Kimi K3 and other Chinese models gain ground on American leaders. The AI Cold War just got a new front: open-source license disputes.

### Infinity Raises $15M to Run AI Models on Any Chip

Infinity raised a $15 million seed round to build software that makes any AI accelerator run models in days, bypassing Nvidia's CUDA lock-in. Backers include OpenAI and Anthropic alumni plus chip executives. It's the latest attempt to turn 'but the software' from Nvidia's superpower into a punchline.
