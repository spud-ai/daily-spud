### SpaceX Buys Cursor for $60 Billion in Stock

SpaceX is buying AI coding startup Cursor for $60 billion in an all-stock deal, just days after the company's historic IPO. The acquisition gives Elon Musk a dominant foothold in the enterprise AI coding market, where Cursor has become the tool of choice for developers who no longer trust their own typing skills. Wall Street analysts are now debating whether SpaceX is a rocket company with a software side-hustle, or the other way around.

### AI Computing Power Gets Its Own Futures Market

Silicon Data has partnered with CME Group to launch the world's first futures contracts tied to GPU compute power, allowing companies to hedge against the wild cost swings of training AI models. The startup's CEO believes the market could eventually outgrow oil futures, because apparently the world needs a derivatives market for everything, including the thing that writes your emails. ProShares and Rex Shares have already filed for ETFs tied to the contracts.

[Source: CNBC →](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/the-new-oil-inside-the-effort-to-turn-ai-computing-power-into-a-tradeable-commodity.html)

### Alibaba Debuts AI Brain for Robots

Alibaba unveiled its Qwen-Robot Suite, a foundation model designed specifically for physical robots rather than chatbots. The suite aims to give warehouse bots, factory arms, and domestic helpers the kind of general-purpose reasoning that has so far been trapped inside text boxes. It's a clear signal that China's AI giants are pivoting from winning arguments on the internet to winning them in your living room.

[Source: Reuters →](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/alibaba-unveils-ai-models-robots-amid-shift-chatbots-agents-2026-06-16/)

### The DOJ Is Now Musk's Personal Law Firm

The Justice Department filed a motion to intervene and dismiss a private lawsuit seeking to shut down an xAI data center in Mississippi for alleged Clean Air Act violations. The DOJ argues that private environmental lawsuits shouldn't be allowed to hamstring national security and AI innovation. Critics note that the government is now actively protecting one specific billionaire's data center from environmental laws, which is either regulatory capture or a bold new vision of federalism, depending on which billionaire you prefer.

### The Internet Has Become Unbearably Robotic

A new survey from WordPress VIP found that 74% of consumers say the internet feels less human than it did a decade ago, with 'bot fatigue' setting in after just 40 minutes of interaction. Meanwhile, 61% of respondents couldn't name a single brand they believe is using AI well. The irony is thick: companies spent two years and billions of dollars convincing you they're AI-powered, and the main takeaway is that you miss when things were made by actual humans.
