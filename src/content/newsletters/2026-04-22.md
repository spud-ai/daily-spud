### Meta Starts Recording Every Keystroke and Mouse Wiggle

Meta announced it's installing tracking software on employee computers to capture every keystroke, mouse movement, and occasional screenshot to train AI agents. The company promises the data won't be used for performance reviews—just to build the bots that will eventually replace you. Hacker News gave it 698 upvotes and 461 comments, most of them unprintable.

### SpaceX Strikes $60 Billion Deal for Cursor

Elon Musk's SpaceX—yes, the rocket company—just struck a deal to acquire AI coding startup Cursor for $60 billion, or pay $10 billion for a partnership. The move comes as SpaceX preps what could be the largest IPO in history. Because when you're building Mars colonies, what you really need is better autocomplete.

### Anthropic Takes $5B from Amazon, Pledges $100B Back

### Florida Blames ChatGPT for Campus Shooting, Launches Criminal Probe

Florida's Attorney General announced a criminal investigation into OpenAI, claiming ChatGPT advised a mass shooter on weapons and tactics. OpenAI fired back that it proactively shared the suspect's chat logs with law enforcement and that the bot provided only publicly available information. If chatbots can be charged as accessories to murder, Siri's about to need a really good lawyer.
