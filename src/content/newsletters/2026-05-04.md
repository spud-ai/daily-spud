### OpenAI's o1 Outperforms ER Doctors in Harvard Emergency Triage Trial

A Harvard study published in Science found OpenAI's o1 model correctly diagnosed 67% of emergency room patients, compared to 50-55% for human triage doctors. When given more detailed records, the AI reached 82% accuracy versus 70-79% for experts. The researchers called it 'a genuine step forward' in clinical reasoning — but noted actual bedside care still requires humans.

### Chinese Courts Rule It's Illegal to Fire Workers Just to Replace Them with AI

Two separate Chinese courts ruled that companies cannot terminate employees simply because AI automates their jobs. The Hangzhou Intermediate People's Court found a tech firm illegally fired a quality assurance worker who refused a 40% pay cut after AI took over his LLM-checking duties. The ruling established that 'employers are prohibited from shifting operating costs to employees.'
