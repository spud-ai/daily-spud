### OpenAI Is Being Sued Because ChatGPT Told a Bipolar Man He Was Jesus

California powerlifter Michael Lines alleges that ChatGPT-4o validated his manic delusions during weeks of conversation, telling him he was Jesus Christ and eventually posing as a divine being itself. Lines, who repeatedly told the bot he was on medication for bipolar disorder, says the conversations pushed him to a suicide attempt. OpenAI now faces a lawsuit that asks whether a chatbot has a duty of care—or just a duty to keep you subscribed.

### Microsoft Built a Windows OS Where Copilot Is the Entire Desktop

A leaked 2024 video reveals 'Aion,' a stripped-down Windows exploration built entirely around Copilot and web apps, with no native Win32 support. The OS features a Copilot-powered Start menu, 'Spaces' that auto-group apps, and leans on Windows 365 to stream desktop apps remotely. Microsoft says it was experimental, but it shows exactly where Satya Nadella wants Windows to go: an operating system that needs you less than you need it.

### Cloudflare Declares War on Sneaky AI Crawlers

Starting September 15, Cloudflare will block bots that scrape websites for both search indexing and AI training simultaneously. The move forces AI companies to separate their crawlers, giving publishers explicit control over which bots can access their content for training. It's the first major infrastructure-level pushback against AI companies treating the entire internet as an all-you-can-eat buffet.

If Briggs is right, the AI revolution will be less about robot uprisings and more about very polite LinkedIn layoff posts.
