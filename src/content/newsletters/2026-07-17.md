### China's Moonshot Just Open-Sourced a 2.8-Trillion-Parameter AI

Moonshot AI dropped Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight model that claims to rival Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol on frontier benchmarks. It's the world's first open 3T-class model and comes with a 1-million-token context window, with full weights dropping July 27. American labs spent years hoarding frontier models; now a Chinese startup is giving one away for free — and Wall Street is suddenly very interested in the word 'open.'

### AI Chatbots Are Helping Repressive Governments Censor Speech

Meta's Oversight Board tested ten major AI models and found they refuse to produce politically critical material 34% of the time for restrictive regimes, versus 14% for free-speech countries. The board says users may be experiencing 'free speech infringements by proxy' as chatbots internalize the speech norms of authoritarian governments. Turns out the safest thing an AI can say about a dictator is absolutely nothing at all.

### OpenAI Made a Physical Keyboard for Coding Agents

OpenAI teamed up with mechanical keyboard maker Work Louder to build Codex Micro, a compact control surface with RGB-lit agent keys, a joystick, and a rotary dial for adjusting reasoning effort. The device acts as a command center for Codex agents, letting developers trigger workflows without touching a mouse. If your keyboard needs its own joystick, maybe your agent is doing too much of the driving.

### Google Rebranded NotebookLM as Gemini Notebook

Google renamed NotebookLM to Gemini Notebook and added a secure cloud computer that can write and execute code inside your research notebooks. The once-simple note-taking tool is now being woven into the Gemini app, Google Search, and Workspace as Google tries to own your entire research workflow. Notebooks finally grew up, got a cloud brain, and joined the Google collective.

### India Just Got Its Second AI Unicorn in a Month

Vibe-coding startup Emergent raised $300 million at a $1.5 billion valuation, becoming India's second AI unicorn in a month after Sarvam hit the same milestone. Emergent says 12 million apps were built on its platform in the last year, 70% by users with no coding experience. India has spent years watching the AI boom from the sidelines; now it is minting unicorns faster than some founders can debug their vibe-generated apps.
